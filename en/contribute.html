Contribute

This site has been designed with data from a few local councils and regions of Cameroon but there is still work to do to complete the image of Cameroon's spending and you can help... Whether you are a local councillor or a concerned citizen, get involved and upload data for your region!

Get Involved

Anyone can contribute their local council's data, visualise it and link it to the map based navigation. The aim is to get as complete a picture of Cameroon's finance as possible, and to keep building this up as a resource, contributing data year on year. Here's how:

Get your data into the OpenSpending Format

Follow the guidelines to learn what you are aiming for. If you want to see what some sample data could look like, take a look at the template.

Some of the modifications can be done in a spreadsheet program such as Excel, however there are more powerful tools which can help with large-scale transformations such as Google Refine. Read the Google Refine tutorial for more information.

Upload your data to the web

Next, you need to get your CSV file online. There are numerous ways to do this, Dropbox, exporting your data directly from a Google Docs (File > Publish to the Web > Publish a CSV). We've used the DataHub to publish our data. If you've never used it before here's a quick demo of how to upload a dataset.

The video below details how this can be done:

Uploading Data to the DataHub from Open Knowledge Foundation on Vimeo.

Import your data into OpenSpending

Next add information about your dataset to make it easily findable in OpenSpending. Again, the video tutorial below walks you through doing this:

OpenSpending Upload: Stage 1 from Open Knowledge Foundation on Vimeo.

Further related information is available on the OpenSpending site.

Create a model for your data

Next, tell OpenSpending how to understand your data by creating a model.

OpenSpending Upload: Step 2 - Create a Model from Open Knowledge Foundation on Vimeo.

If that's all gone well, you'll be able to move on to creating visualisations (video coming soon - watch this space!) and if not and you have some errors watch the video below to see what to do next.

Fix any errors

It doesn't always go right the first time you try and load your data - here's what to do if you have made a mistake either in your model or your data.

How to Fix Errors in OpenSpending from Open Knowledge Foundation on Vimeo.

Visualisations

Now you are finished, and visualisations will automatically be generated from the data you uploaded into OpenSpending. All you have to do is:

  1. Link up the data to Cameroon.OpenSpending.org. There is a tutorial on this here
  2. If you are adding data for a new region of Cameroon (as opposed to new data relating to a new financial year for an area of Cameroon which already has a map selector) - you will need to create a new sub-national map selector. Instructions on how to do this can be found here.

Questions?

If you have any technical questions about adding data to the site, please feel free to contact the OpenSpending team
